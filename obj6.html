Network Security Objective 6

Research, document, test and evaluate several current industry

information security based threats, risks, malicious activities,

covert methodology, encryption technologies, mitigation techniques

or unconventional tactics to prevent loss of sensitive information

and data confidentiality, integrity and availability.

Objective Application

With this objective I had to revisit some previous assignments as well
as a different one from my NTS330 Applied Exploits and Hacking class.
This class was designed to present the fundamental and historical
perspective of hacking methodologies and applied hands-on skills with
the various assignments.

The previous assignments I mentioned were my Pentest Lab Setup and my
Recon assignment.

The Pentest Lab Setup had me setting up a pentest platform and documenting
my process every step of the way. This assignment helped me to familiarize
myself with the tools and methodology for testing and evaluating various
kinds of information security based threats currently plaguing the industry.

The Recon assignment had me gathering information and documenting my
findings on an organization through various data gathering means other
than direct scanning. This allowed me to evaluate first hand how potenial
attacks could be conducted with current covert methodologies being used.

The new assignment that I visited for this objective was
a research paper on an exploit that I was allowed to select.

In my Exploit Research and Presentation assignment I selected the
Ripple20 vulnerabilities. Ripple20 is a series of 19 different
zero-day vulnerabilities, discovered back in June of 2020 that were
directly affecting IoT devices and was considered a wide spread issue
(this class taking place in October of 2020). This research paper
provided me with the experiance in discovering, researching, and
documenting current vulnerabilities and exploits.

These assignments showcase my completion of this objective with
the provided pen testing guides and research on current exploits.
